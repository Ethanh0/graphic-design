Typography Project!

Axial

Axial arranges elements on either side of a line or axis. Axial typographic systems are not always used on their own and can be combined with other typographic systems to make an engaging effect with the viewer.

In my image different blocks of text are arranged opposite each other, this is how the typographic system is used to draw in an audience.

Bilateral

Bilateral arranges all the elements by centering them in the middle of the page. It is one of the more basic systems but gets straight to the point

My example image does exactly this. I added lines inbetween the paragraphs to make it a little more interesting.

Dilational

The dilational typographic system involves one or many center points where the text wraps around these points making an almost ripple effect

My created image has several of these center points and although the points are irregular its a satisfying effect that will attract a viewers attention.

Grid

The grid system as the name suggests organises the contained elements into a grid like shape. There can be visible or invisible margins that seperate the content from each other.

In my image there are no visible margins but you can tell the text is organised into a specific grid-like area. I chose to place the text next to this black and yellow symbol just to make the image more pleasant and engaging.

Modular

In a modular typographic system elements are organised and arranged in a grid or modular structure, this is a more structured approach than "dilational" for example but at the same time it is unintresting and not the most creative.

My approach to this system involved placing the information in blocks that follow a grid-like structure. I alternated the colors to make the design more appealing.

Radial

Radial elements are arranged to look like they are coming out of a centeral focal point. Radial systems can be hard to read sometimes as as different lines of text are being written at different angles.

In my design i chose to only use text that reads from left to right so it is easy to read but remains interesting to an audience or viewer, like i previously mentioned this is not always the case and text can be read from multiple different angles.

Random

As the name suggests the random typographic system follows no pattern or structure, there can be varied font sizes, overlapping and layering and an all out "random" structure.

In my image text is split up, rotated to different angles and varies in font size.

Transitional

Transitional arranges elements into textured layers with varying tones, the layers arent always neatly alligned giving it a more relaxed feel

In my image it looks almost like an atmosphere is being created by wind blowing the text across the page, this makes the piece feel soothing to look at and satisfies the viewer.

All in all i enjoyed this part of the project, learning about the typographic systems was fun and i liked creating my own versions of each system for the specific information the task gave me. coming up with ideas of what to do for each system was challenging at times but thinking of my own design for each one helped me get a deeper understanding of how each individual system works and is used in graphic design.

If i was to do this again i would spend longer on my own typographic system designs and make them look as high quality and profesional as i can.
